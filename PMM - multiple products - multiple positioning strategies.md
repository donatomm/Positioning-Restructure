If you have multiple products...

You have multiple positioning strategies.

But what do most multi-product startups do with their positioning?

They try to build a one-size-fits all positioning and messaging strategy.

Which is the perfect strategy if you want everyone to be equally disappointed. 😥

Take [[HubSpot]{.underline}](https://www.linkedin.com/company/hubspot/) for example.

On the surface, it looks like one unified product suite, but look closer:

• Marketing Hub: Targeted at marketers who want to attract and convert leads.

• Sales Hub: Built for sales teams to streamline outreach and close deals.

• Service Hub: Geared toward support teams focused on delivering great customer service.

(And this is just to name a few, they also have CommerceHub, ContentHub, and Operations Hub)

Each product has its own unique positioning and needs to speak directly to a unique champion: a marketer, a sales rep, or a customer support manager.

And here's the kicker.

Because the positioning is different, each product requires a discrete GTM strategy.

The messaging, channels, and sales playbooks must be tailored for each audience.

And let's not underestimate the operational complexity:

👉 Different sales enablement materials

👉 Separate onboarding experiences

👉 Unique success metrics for each product

------

The Bottom line:

A multi-product strategy brings massive opportunities - BUT ONLY if you're ready to tackle the positioning and operational challenges.

And I'm sorry to be the one to tell you...

Your 10-person startup with \$5M dollars of funding can't handle these challenges.
