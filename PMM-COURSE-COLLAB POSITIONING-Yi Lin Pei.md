Most positioning and messaging don't fail because the PMM doesn't know April Dunford's framework. They fail because the work is done in a silo.... which means:

It never got full buy-in and therefore won't stick.

My solution: Make positioning collaborative from Day 1

To do this, I run a workshop in [Miro](https://www.linkedin.com/company/mirohq/) with the CEO, functional heads (product, sales, CS, marketing), and a few people with deep knowledge of the business.

Here's the exact flow (which I map out in a Miro board like below):

1️⃣ Start with real customers who love you

I ask the group: "Who are the customers that get the most value from us?"

This is where the first debate usually happens.

➡️ Some leaders realize their ICP definition is vague.

➡️ Others discover they don't fully agree on the "best-fit" segment.

That tension is the point. Until we have consensus here, the rest of the messaging will fall apart.

2️⃣ Narrow to the champion persona

Once we agree on the ICP, I ask: "Within that customer, who is our champion?"

There is usually a discussion here: is it the end user? The budget holder? The exec sponsor? You can't build sharp positioning without knowing who the champion is.

3️⃣ Dig into their jobs-to-be-done

With the champion persona clear, we go deeper to understand:

What motivates them?

What are they trying to achieve?

What's blocking them today?

4️⃣ Define the competitive alternative

I ask: "If our product didn't exist, how would they solve this problem today?"

The answers might range from another vendor, a manual process, or doing nothing. But picking ONE is critical. You can't position against everything, so you need a sharp reference point.

5️⃣ Surface differentiators

I ask: "What makes us different from that alternative?"

This step usually leads to these patterns:

Some "differentiators" turn out to be table stakes.

Others are things only one team knew about.

And a few gems rise to the top as true competitive strengths.

6️⃣ Build the value matrix (after the workshop)

I take the prioritized differentiators and build a value matrix that includes:

Attribute → Capability → Benefit → Business Outcome

This connects the dots from product details to the impact the champion actually cares about.

7️⃣ Create the messaging house

Finally, I synthesize everything into a messaging house:

ICP & champion persona

Key challenges / jobs-to-be-done

Differentiators & value pillars

Proof points & business outcomes

This document becomes the foundation for all GTM content. And because it came out of a collaborative workshop, the whole company feels ownership of it.
