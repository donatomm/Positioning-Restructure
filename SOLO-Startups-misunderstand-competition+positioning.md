[Startups in emerging markets often misunderstand what they're actually competing against.\
]{.mark}

[TL;DR\
In early markets your competitor is often the status quo, not venture-backed startups going after the same space.]{.mark}

[Positioning against rival venture-backed startups going after the same space.]{.mark}

[This is the FOUNDER'S view on the competition.]{.mark}

[But the founder's view doesn't matter.]{.mark}

[It's the CUSTOMER'S view that matters.]{.mark}

[\-\--]{.mark}

[In **immature** markets, the customer isn't even aware this new type of solution exists, let alone who all the up-and-coming players are.]{.mark}

- [So when a founder tries to message to them leading with: "We're the best (insert new category name)", it makes no freakin' sense to the customer.]{.mark}

- [It'd be like trying to sell a smart phone to someone living in rural Asia who doesn't even have a landline.]{.mark}

- [Founder: "We're the best smart phone!"]{.mark}

- [Prospect: "What's a smart phone?"]{.mark}

[[[Vanta]{.underline}](https://www.linkedin.com/company/vanta-security/) (circa 2018) is the perfect B2B example of this:]{.mark}

[When they launched, there were already other startups building "SOC 2 automation platforms."]{.mark}

[From a founder's perspective, the competition looked like:]{.mark}

[• Tugboat Logic]{.mark}

[• ControlMap]{.mark}

[• Reciprocity]{.mark}

[• (and a few others)]{.mark}

[So the natural instinct would've been:]{.mark}

["We need to position against these other SOC 2 automation vendors."]{.mark}

[But here's the reality of the situation in 2018:]{.mark}

[Most companies weren't shopping for "SOC 2 automation platforms."]{.mark}

[They were trying to complete a SOC 2 audit.]{.mark}

[The real competition wasn't other venture-backed startups.]{.mark}

[It was:]{.mark}

[• Spreadsheets]{.mark}

[• Google Drive folders]{.mark}

[• Endless screenshot collection]{.mark}

[• Expensive compliance consultants]{.mark}

[If Vanta had led with:]{.mark}

["We're the best SOC 2 automation platform."]{.mark}

[The response would've been:]{.mark}

["What's a SOC 2 automation platform?"]{.mark}

[Instead, the winning framing is much simpler:]{.mark}

["Stop managing your SOC 2 audit in spreadsheets."]{.mark}

[That lands.]{.mark}

[Because it speaks to the job the customer is trying to get done --- not the category the founder wishes existed.]{.mark}

[---------]{.mark}

[In almost every immature market, you are not fighting direct competitors.]{.mark}

[You are fighting the status quo.]{.mark}

[Message accordingly.]{.mark}
