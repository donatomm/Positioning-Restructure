[I see a few possible causes (except "wrong product") 🙂]{.mark}

(1) [Wrong Positioning. Are we conveying the differentiated value through a compelling story?]{.mark}

(2) [Wrong Audience. Are we using the right way to sory-tell value to the right audience/Persona? E.g. Way too technical or way too hi-level?]{.mark}

(3) [Wrong time. Does the product/Value reflect the solution of a problem that is urgent and pervasive enough now? E.g. are we too early (see the Law of Diffusion of Innovation)]{.mark}

(4) [The pitch has been the same for months? Always try to test it with variations to see what works best]{.mark}

(5) [Is the story you are telling Remarkable and Compelling?]{.mark}

(6) [Have you refined the pitch (positioning) with sales?]{.mark}

[Most sales pitches aren\'t weak due to a lack of expertise or product understanding - they\'re dying of neglect.]{.mark}

[I\'ve seen a LOT of sales pitches, and here\'s the pattern I see:]{.mark}

[1/ The basics of the pitch were created years ago, and it\'s been tinkered with hundreds of times until it has become a Frankenstein monster of little pieces mashed together. There\'s no consistent positioning, no compelling story thread that holds it together.]{.mark}

[2/ The structure and flow of the pitch are an afterthought. A product walkthrough demo might form the backbone of the pitch and often even that doesn\'t have a structure beyond - hey let me show you all the things.]{.mark}

[3/ Nobody really \"owns\" making the pitch great, so nobody has the time and inclination to blow it up and start from scratch. When it needs updating, it bounces around like a hot potato until someone adds a little piece, and then\...we get point number 1.]{.mark}

[We are left with a first-call pitch deck that isn\'t doing the positioning or the story justice for prospects. An experienced sales rep might not care too much - they\'ve done enough deals to know what works and what doesn\'t - but for everyone else? We could do a whole lot better.]{.mark}
