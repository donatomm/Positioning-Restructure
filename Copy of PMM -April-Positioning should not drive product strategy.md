Positioning should not drive product strategy - it should work the other way around. The company has a vision for the future, there is a strategy that defines the steps the company is going to take to achieve the vision and there is positioning that defines how the company wins business in the market at every step in that strategy.

If let our positioning drive product strategy, we will run into two problems:

1/ Our product strategy will always be rooted in the present or the past. Our positioning defines how we win in the market today. We can (and often should) build a roadmap that helps to strengthen the value we currently deliver. However, in tech, over the lifetime of a successful product, the value will shift into other places. For example - today you deliver Value X for customers but in 5 years you will have enough data to deliver Value Y. You cannot sell Y today, and looking at X in isolation doesn\'t give you a plan to get to Y. If positioning is driving you roadmap you are going to be orienting around value X forever. For some products, that is perfectly fine but for many (particularly in tech) this leave you open to being leap-frogged by competitors and new entrants.

2/ We are orienting our marketing and sales messages around future promises that we cannot deliver on today. I\'ve seen companies get way out over their skis trying to orient their positioning around the vision of where they will be in the future only to discover that customers don\'t want to pay today\'s dollars for tomorrow\'s promises. Creating urgency requires you to build a strong case for why a customer needs to move on a deal right now - not a year from now.
