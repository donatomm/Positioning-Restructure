# The Fletch Framework: Positioning & Anchor Strategy

## 1. What is Positioning?

Positioning is not a tagline or a logo. According to the Fletch methodology, positioning is:

> **\"The process of helping a customer mentally classify what your product or service is.\"**

If a customer cannot mentally classify your product, they cannot shop for it, budget for it, or understand why they need it. Effective positioning removes the cognitive load from the customer by providing a clear mental \"hook.\"

## 2. The Competitive Trap: Rivals vs. Status Quo

Many startups fail because \"Founder fear and ego kick in,\" leading them to position against rival software vendors rather than the actual alternative.

### The Problem with Competitive Messaging

When founders default to \"me-too\" messaging, they focus on being the \"most advanced\" or \"fastest.\" However:

> **\"If your customers have never heard of your category\... don\'t position against rival software vendors. Instead position for what they\'re trying to do.\"**

## 6. How-To: Step-by-Step Methodology

Follow these steps to move from \"Founder Ego\" messaging to \"Capability-Anchored\" clarity.

### Step 1: Define the Job-To-Be-Done (JTBD)

Identify what the customer would say they are trying to do in plain English.

- **The Question:** \"What is the specific outcome the customer is seeking?\"

- **Verification:** If the customer wouldn\'t use your category name to describe their goal (e.g., \"I need a SOC 2 Audit,\" not \"I need a compliance automation platform\"), use their words.

### Step 2: Audit the Status Quo Alternatives

List how the customer currently solves the problem without software.

- **Look for:** High-cost consultants, manual spreadsheets, or \"hacky\" internal processes.

- **Outcome:** This is your *actual* competition. Your messaging must attack the \"tedious, complex, or expensive\" nature of these specific alternatives.

### Step 3: Select Your Primary Anchors

Choose the anchors that provide a **\"sufficient definition of the product in isolation.\"**

- **Category Anchor:** Defines what you are (e.g., \"Product Marketing Consultancy\").

- **Use Case Anchor:** Defines what you do (e.g., \"Position your product\").

- **Alternative Anchor:** Defines what you replace (e.g., \"Alternative to Brand Agencies\").

- **Test:** Ask, \"Does this anchor sufficiently answer \'What is![](media/image2.png){width="6.458333333333333in" height="0.7175929571303588in"}\
  ?\' by itself?\" If the answer is too vague (e.g., \"Service business\"), it is not a primary anchor.

### Step 4: Map the Problem to the Anchor

Connect a specific pain point to your primary anchor to create resonance.

- **Logic:** \"Positioning is difficult![](media/image1.png){width="6.236166885389326in" height="0.749588801399825in"}\
  because teams are too close to the product and lose objectivity![](media/image4.png){width="6.458333333333333in" height="0.7175929571303588in"}\
  .\"

### Step 5: Define Differentiation Pillars

Identify the 2-3 core reasons why your solution is better than the status quo.

- **Focus:** How do you solve the specific sub-problems identified in Step 4? (e.g., \"External Objectivity,\" \"Battle-tested Methodology\").

### Step 6: Translate to Capability-Anchored Messaging

Convert your high-level benefits into concrete capabilities.

- **The Formula:** Focus on the **Tool** or **Action** (The \"How\") rather than the **Dream** (The \"Why\").

- **Example:** Move from \"Boost Productivity\" (Benefit) to \"Connect your CRM to LinkedIn\" (Capability).

## 7. From Anchors to Strategy: The Pitch

The final step is combining these elements into a cohesive narrative:

1.  **Category/Use Case Anchor** (What you are/do)

2.  **Persona Anchor** (Who you help)

3.  **Problem Summary** (The pain you solve)

4.  **Differentiation Summary** (Why you are better)

## Conclusion

Positioning fails when it is built on ego or abstract benefits. By identifying the **Status Quo** as the true competitor and anchoring the message in a **Concrete Capability**, startups achieve market adoption by enabling clear mental classification.
