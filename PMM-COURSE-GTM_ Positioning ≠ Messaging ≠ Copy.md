\
\
\
\
I\'ve rewritten landing page copy more times than I want to admit. And for a while, I thought that was just part of the job. Keep testing until eventually something sticks.\
\
But some wise mentors course-corrected my lost ship with these lessons:\
\
\[𝟭\] 𝗣𝗼𝘀𝗶𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗶𝘀𝗻\'𝘁 𝗮 𝗺𝗮𝗿𝗸𝗲𝘁𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗲𝘅𝗲𝗿𝗰𝗶𝘀𝗲\
\
The first time I tried to fix a product\'s positioning, I did it in a doc, on the fly. Needless to say, it didn\'t end well.\
\
Turned out, positioning is a business identity decision, not a marketing artifact.\
\
\"Think of the market as a mental warehouse of boxes.\"\
\
Positioning is the exercise where you decide:\
- which box you want to be placed in or\
- whether you\'ll create a new one altogether and\
- how you\'ll stand out in that box wrt everything else\
\
It\'s also a raging battle against an identity crisis.\
\
\"Are we a CRM? A database? A martech tool?\
Ice cream?\"\
\
Also, if the CEO and revenue leaders aren\'t making that call with you, then it\'ll die pretty soon.\
\
I\'m not saying you need a 20-slide pptx with a positioning statement collecting dust. But you need a shared understanding of where you play in the market.\
\
\
\[𝟮\] 𝗬𝗼𝘂 𝗰𝗮𝗻\'𝘁 𝗱𝗶𝗿𝗲𝗰𝘁𝗹𝘆 𝘄𝗿𝗶𝘁𝗲 𝗰𝗼𝗽𝘆\
\
It\'s tempting to throw prompts at ChatGPT and iterate on headlines.\
\
But what are you feeding it as context?\
\
Without a grounded message, I was just picking copy that sounded \"interesting\" but lacked substance or truth.\
\
This meant answering the hard questions:\
- What capability matters most?\
- What is the primary benefit?\
- What\'s the main use case?\
- Why are we different?\
\
Moreover, what\'s our unique worldview about what\'s broken and needs fixing?\
\
\
\[𝟯\] 𝗠𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗮𝗴𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗶𝘀𝗻\'𝘁 𝗲𝗻𝗼𝘂𝗴𝗵\
\
This one took me the longest to figure out.\
\
Q: If I have the \"messaging\", why can\'t I just copy and paste it everywhere?\
\
Well, messaging is the substance. The core pitch.\
\
But it\'s in GRAYSCALE.\
\
It lacks brand personality and tone.\
\
It also lacks context.\
\
A social ad has different demands than a landing page and a sales deck.\
\
And here\'s something no one told me:\
Messaging allows you to scale.\
\
For every vector of messaging, you can have many variations of copy.\
\
This means you can give a team of copywriters (or multiple agencies) one messaging brief, and you\'ll get a diversity of copy expressions that won\'t lose the plot.\
\
It sounds complicated. Sometimes it is.\
\
But all three components play an important part in getting comms right. Trust the process.
