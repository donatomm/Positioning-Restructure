## In early startups, a product may be positioned against the wrong competitor, making the message irrelevant.

### Key Insights

- This approach overlooks the fact that the customer might not know the category exists, resulting in \"worthless\" messaging from the customer\'s perspective.

- Customers who don\'t know about a company\'s rivals are not the company\'s competition.

- If customers haven\'t heard of the category, a company should not position against rival software vendors.

### The Correct Positioning Strategy:

- A company should instead position for \"The Job-to-Be-Done\" (JTB), which is what customers are actually trying to accomplish.

  - In the Vanta example, the Job-to-Be-Done is \"Completing SOC 2 Audit\". This is what the customer would say they are trying to do.

- The actual competition is the \"status quo way\" customers address the JTB.

  - For the SOC 2 audit, the competition is the compliance consultant (\"We can handle your SOC 2 audit for \$100k\") and \"Doing the work in-house (w/ spreadsheets)\", where the customer might feel it is \"way more work than I thought\".

- This status quo is the real competition, not rival software vendors like Drata or ControlMap.

- Speaking against the status quo competition will make the message resonate.

### The Problem with Competitive Positioning:

- It uses the example of Vanta launching its SOC 2 compliance product in 2018.

- Seeing other \"SOC 2 Automation Platforms\" led to messaging focused on positioning against these competitors.

- Examples of competitive messaging included:

  - \"We're the most advanced SOC 2 Automation Platform\".

  - \"We're the YC-Backed SOC 2 Automation Platform\".

  - \"We're the SOC 2 Automation Platform built for modern teams\".

  - \"We're the fastest SOC 2 Automation Platform\".

  - \"We're the most comprehensive SOC 2 Automation Platform\".

  - \"We're the premium SOC 2 Automation Platform\".
