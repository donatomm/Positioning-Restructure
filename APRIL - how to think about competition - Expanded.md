## OUTLINES

I. General Approach to Competition:

- Consider competition by naming approaches rather than specific products or companies.

- Categorize competitors into buckets based on their approaches to solving customer needs.

- Position competitors in these buckets to simplify the competitive landscape.

II\. Rule for Market Leaders:

- Market leaders should avoid explicitly naming smaller competitors in marketing or sales materials.

- Mentioning smaller competitors may legitimize them and divert customer attention, potentially affecting the purchasing decision.

- (DM note) You don't want to be associated with smaller, less comprehensive, less powerful competitors, because buyers will then compare you with them, forgetting your actual uniqueness, and looking at a price comparison that will damage you

III\. Example of Microsoft and Slack:

- Microsoft\'s mention of Slack during the launch of Teams had mixed results.

- Slack\'s response through a full-page ad may have unintentionally legitimized Microsoft as a competitor.

- The move created confusion and made it seem like Microsoft was launching a product similar to Slack.

IV\. Small Companies Naming Big Competitors:

- Small companies should name the dominant player in the market if customers are already considering them.

- Acknowledging the big competitor can enhance credibility and show awareness of the market dynamics.

V. Positioning Competitors:

- Never miss an opportunity to position competitors and shape the narrative.

- Positioning should be rooted in facts and integrity, highlighting real differences without resorting to misinformation.

VI\. Responding to Competitors\' Lies:

- Combat competitors\' lies with a strong positioning strategy based on truth and integrity.

- Clarify the truth and show where competitors may be stretching it, building trust with customers.

VII\. Giving Ground and Defending Territory:

- Admitting shortcomings in some areas can enhance credibility, but focus on strengths and unique value propositions.

- Tight positioning provides the tools to defend territory and confidently navigate competitive challenges.

VIII\. Final Thoughts:

- Maintain a strong and tight positioning strategy to confidently navigate competition.

- Consider specific tactics based on the company\'s size, market position, and the competitive landscape.
