**FletchPMM Positioning Anchors Framework: Content Guide**

**Overview**

Anthony Pierri and Rob Kaminski, co-founders of FletchPMM (Fletch), have developed one of the most widely referenced B2B SaaS positioning frameworks in the product marketing space. Their methodology centers on the concept of **positioning anchors** --- strategic reference points that help explain a product\'s value clearly to target buyers. Across 500+ client engagements, they\'ve evolved their framework from a simple canvas to a sophisticated system built around **primary anchors**, **secondary anchors**, and a **differentiation summary**.\[1\]\[2\]\[3\]

The specific terminology in question --- Company Anchor, Persona Anchor, Use Case Anchor, Alternative Anchor, Category Anchor, and Differentiation Summary --- appears throughout Fletch\'s positioning methodology and has been adopted, discussed, and adapted by the broader PMM community.

**Core Content from FletchPMM**

**Positioning & Messaging Canvas (July 2025 --- Latest Version)**

Anthony Pierri shared the latest version of Fletch\'s Positioning & Messaging Canvas in July 2025, which introduced significant methodological updates. This version operationalizes the concept of **primary anchors** and **secondary anchors** and shifts from a \"capability-first\" to a \"differentiation-first\" messaging approach.\[1\]

- **Source:** [LinkedIn post by Anthony Pierri (July 2025)](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anthonypierri_positioning-messaging-canvas-activity-7353454698890194944-epC8)\[1\]

- **Also hosted at:** [FletchPMM Resource: Positioning & Messaging Canvas July 2025](https://www.fletchpmm.com/resource/positioning-messaging-canvas-july-2025)\[4\]

Key updates include the ability to build a positioning narrative at three levels of depth --- a one-sentence company description, a longer elevator pitch, and a full homepage-length story.\[1\]

**The World\'s Simplest Positioning Equation (2025 Update)**

This resource distills the Fletch framework to its core: **Positioning = Target Customer + Your Differentiation**. The target customer side includes firmographic and persona-based elements (Company Anchor, Persona Anchor), while the differentiation side includes what the product is (Category Anchor or Use Case Anchor), what it replaces (Alternative Anchor), and why it\'s better (Differentiation Summary).\[5\]\[6\]

- **Source:** [FletchPMM Resource](https://www.fletchpmm.com/resource/the-world-s-simplest-positioning-equation-2025-update)\[5\]

- **LinkedIn version:** [Anthony Pierri post (January 2025)](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anthonypierri_productmarketing-saas-b2b-activity-7284585014342643712-KBvq)\[6\]

**Growth Unhinged: The Definitive Product Positioning Framework (6,500-word series)**

**Part 1 --- Choosing Your Target Customer Segment** covers how to define who the product is for, including Company Anchor (firmographics like industry, size, business model) and Persona Anchor (the champion closest to the problem).\[9\]

**Douwe Wester: \"I Kept Rewriting My Clients\' Messaging\...\"**

This is one of the few posts that explicitly uses all the anchor terminology from the query. It provides a step-by-step process inspired by FletchPMM: map 12 positioning elements organized into **secondary anchors** (ICP, Personas, Use Case) and **primary anchors** (Alternative, Category), then write the functional frame: *\"\[Product\] is a \[category anchor\] that helps \[persona anchor\] in \[company anchor\] \[use case anchor\].\"* It specifies that each primary anchor should answer \"What is \[product\]?\" on its own --- if it can\'t, it\'s secondary.\[16\]

- **Source:** [LinkedIn post by Douwe Wester (February 2026)](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/douwewester_i-kept-rewriting-my-clients-messaging-because-activity-7432346689266642944-R2_T)\[16\]

**Anchor Terminology Reference**

The following table maps the anchor terms from the query to their role in the Fletch framework:

  ----------------------------- ----------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Anchor Term                   Role                          Description

  **Company Anchor**            Secondary (Target Customer)   Firmographic criteria --- industry, company size, business model --- defining the ideal company type\[21\]\[16\]

  **Persona Anchor**            Secondary (Target Customer)   The specific person within the company closest to the problem --- the \"champion\"\[2\]\[16\]

  **Use Case Anchor**           Primary                       A customer activity, workflow, or process the product addresses --- best for early/emerging markets\[11\]\[12\]

  **Alternative Anchor**        Primary                       The current way customers accomplish the task without the product (a competing tool, a manual process, spreadsheets)\[10\]\[23\]

  **Category Anchor**           Primary                       A recognized product category buyers already search for and compare (e.g., \"CRM,\" \"design tool\")\[11\]\[12\]

  **Differentiation Summary**   Positioning output            The combined statement of how the product stands out --- binary differentiation (\"they do X, we do Y\") or differentiation by degree (\"faster, cheaper, easier\")\[10\]\[16\]
  ----------------------------- ----------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The key distinction in the framework: **Primary anchors** answer \"What is this product?\" on their own. **Secondary anchors** (Company, Persona) add specificity but cannot independently classify the product. Leading with only secondary anchors is the most common positioning mistake --- resulting in vague messaging that leaves buyers confused.\[24\]\[23\]
