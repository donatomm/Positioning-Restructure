# Positioning and Visibility Systems in the Age of AI

**Source:** Michel Fortin, Head of Growth at Consulting Success®

### The Foundation of Strategic Visibility

Three decades ago, I sat across from my first client, a plastic surgeon who was brilliant but invisible. Their expertise went unnoticed, siloed by "if you build it, they will come" thinking.

That experience became my wake-up call, and it set the foundation for my career as a positioning expert and growth strategist obsessed with visibility. (It even led to me writing my first book, \"The 10 Commandments of Power Positioning,\" back in \'92.)

Whether leading cross-functional teams for global SaaS brands or working hands-on with boutique firms, I've learned that organic growth isn't about random acts of marketing. It's about **engineering presence and amplifying it** to achieve prominence.

### Visibility as a System

The real game-changer? Treating visibility as a system, not a tactic. Take another client, a digital learning platform, I helped scale their online presence. By revamping core content architecture and optimizing for authority signals, we boosted organic traffic by 244% and visibility by 79%.

When you create intentional content ecosystems, identify gaps, and leverage emerging tech, you don't just drive traffic. **You become the obvious choice.** One tip I share with every client: Visibility isn't built by shouting louder. It\'s built by delivering deeper value to the right audience.

### The "Visibility Multiplier Effect"

- **Audit your content, audience channels, and competitors** with an outcome-focused approach. Where are you visible, and what's seen as valuable? Where are your competitors visible and you\'re not?

- **Optimize for both humans and algorithms.** Today\'s SEO isn't just keywords. It's topical authority, user experience, and relevance. Focus on providing value, and engines will love you for it.

- **Amplify strategic insights with AI** using market intelligence. Today, AI can help surface content gaps, emerging trends, and customer pain points faster than any human team.

- **Invest in frameworks and systems** that compound your expertise, so every lesson learned, client win, and strategic insight continues to deliver value and fuels your future growth over time.

After orchestrating hundreds of million-dollar campaigns and mentoring thousands of business professionals, I know success is never a single headline or viral post. It's the result of consistent, value-driven visibility that puts your authority in the right place at the right time.

### The Evolution of the Internet and AI

In 1992, I built my first client website. Most people had no idea what the internet even was. When I explained it, they\'d stare blankly. \"Why would anyone want that?\"

A few years later, I stood in front of a classroom at Algonquin College of Applied Arts and Technology teaching something called \"ecommerce.\" Brand new course. Brand new concept. The college was known for being innovative, so they took a chance on it. I remember writing on that whiteboard, trying to explain why businesses would eventually sell things online. Some students got it. Others looked at me like I was describing science fiction.

I was early. Really early. And here\'s what I learned: **being early didn\'t matter as much as I thought it would.**

### Being First in the Mind

Fast forward to today. Everyone\'s racing to adopt AI. The fear of missing out is palpable. \"If I\'m not using AI, I\'ll be left behind.\" Well, I\'ve watched this movie before. Here\'s what three decades of marketing have taught me about being first. **Being first in the marketplace is not nearly as important as being first in the mind.**

Microsoft and Google rarely create new categories. They copy them from others. Yet they dominate. The reason is, they deploy quickly, at mass-market scale, and become first in the minds of their customers. The original innovator becomes a footnote. The same thing is happening with AI right now.

Everyone\'s rushing to use the same tools, generate the same content, chase the same tactics. They\'re creating more noise, not more signal. But here\'s the positioning truth I\'ve relied on since I wrote Power Positioning: **Uniqueness separates you from the competition instead of comparing you to them.**

As I wrote back then (and it\'s applicable today and even more so), don\'t duplicate. Differentiate! In 2026 and beyond, I believe that the winners won\'t be those who adopted AI first. They\'ll be those who used AI to amplify a focused position that already lived in their audience\'s mind.

### The Laser Focus Strategy

Think of it like a laser. The smaller and more focused the beam, the more powerful it becomes. AI should tighten your focus, not scatter it. So before you ask \"How do I use AI?\" ask this instead: **What do I want to be first in the mind for?**

If you can\'t answer that clearly, no amount of AI tools will save you. You\'ll just be adding to the noise. Start by thinking about what makes you different. Or what you can make different. Sometimes you have to manufacture a position that didn\'t exist before.

### 2026 Market Shifts: Survival vs. Irrelevance

As I look at what\'s unfolding in 2026, I see five market shifts that will separate those who survive from those who become irrelevant.

1.  **The First-in-Mind Fulcrum:** Most companies are still fighting to be the loudest in their markets. But in 2026, they\'ll lose. The old playbook of mass content creation to capture more keywords, more eyeballs, and more likes is dying. AI results are forcing us back to fundamentals. Positioning wins over promotion.

2.  **The AI Productivity Paradox:** Companies betting on AI to 10x their output will discover they\'ve actually 10x\'d their irrelevance. The winners will be those who used AI to amplify focused expertise, not replace strategic thinking. AI should scale your unique methodology, not generate generic fluff or \"expertise slop.\"

3.  **The Death of Diluted Expertise:** AI democratizes basic knowledge. Being \"good at marketing\" or \"experienced in SEO\" becomes worthless. You need to become a recognized authority. 2026 brings brutal natural selection. Only hyper-specialized positioning will survive.

4.  **The Resilience Realignment:** Between economic uncertainty and AI-driven layoffs, clients are tightening their purse strings. People want solid foundations. Your 2026 positioning needs to answer: \"How do you help me survive this?\" not \"How do you help me 10x?\"

5.  **The Authority Arbitrage:** Experts have the opportunity to use AI to document their unique methodologies and scale their thought leadership. Those who move now build moats. Those who wait become commodities.

**Conclusion:** Stop trying to be loudest. Stop trying to produce more. Stop trying to be everywhere. Instead, become first in mind for something specific. Because in 2026, the question isn\'t \"How much content can I create?\" It\'s \"What mental real estate do I own?\"
