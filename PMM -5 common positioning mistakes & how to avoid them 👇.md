[5 common positioning mistakes & how to avoid them 👇]{.mark}

[]{.mark}

[Positioning can make or break your GTM strategy. As a marketing leader, avoiding these mistakes is critical:]{.mark}

[]{.mark}

[1️⃣ Siloed process: You are creating your new positioning without collaborating with other revenue teams. So don\'t treat it like a solo marketing project.]{.mark}

[2️⃣ Ignoring the customer POV: Positioning that doesn\'t start with the customer\'s perspective is doomed to fail. Understand their jobs to be done and their problems, how and with what they currently solve them, and where your solution fits in.]{.mark}

[3️⃣ Broad positioning: Trying to appeal to everyone often means appealing to no one. Focus on specific segments and tailor your message to resonate deeply with them.]{.mark}

[4️⃣ Neglect market context: Your solution needs a relevant frame of reference. Use market trends to position your product in a context that makes sense to your audience.]{.mark}

[5️⃣ Static positioning: As your company grows and your ICPs evolve, such as moving upmarket, so must your positioning. Review and refine it regularly to stay relevant.]{.mark}

[]{.mark}

[Proper positioning is the cornerstone of a holistic and successful GTM strategy. Only with the right framing and differentiation based on value creation for your target audience can you truly win the growth game. 🚀]{.mark}

[Pro tips for effective positioning:]{.mark}

[☑ No positioning statement: If you\'re following a strategic positioning process, a generic positioning statement will most likely create unclear messages.]{.mark}

[☑ Highlight unique value: Differentiate your solution by emphasizing what makes it truely unique and why that matters to your customers.]{.mark}

[☑ Category Design: If (and only if) you are truly solving a clear problem in a new way, let the world know and create a new category. Then start changing the way your ICP thinks by promoting your \"new way\".]{.mark}

[☑ Segment Focus: Positioning should be targeted to specific customer segments, not a one-size-fits-all approach. Use the right frame of reference of your specific segment and ICP.]{.mark}

[☑ Documentation: Keep a clear, documented positioning strategy accessible to all teams. Educate them properly. Then, for example, your marketing team can create aligned messaging.]{.mark}

[☑ Sales Pitch: Documenting your positioning and creating new messaging is not enough, you also need a sales story and new pitch decks.]{.mark}
