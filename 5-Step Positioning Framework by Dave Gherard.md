#### **5-Step Positioning Framework by DAVE G**

Exit Five Founder. CMO in a past life.

Positioning your startup effectively is crucial for standing out in a crowded market. Here\'s a straightforward framework you can use to develop your marketing positioning.

## The 5-Step Positioning Framework

### 1. Identify Your Target Audience

- Problem Statement: Who are you selling to?

- Solution: Define your ideal customer profile (ICP). Get specific about demographics, psychographics, and behavior. Create buyer personas if needed.

### 2. Analyze the Competition

- Problem Statement: What are your competitors doing?

- Solution: Conduct a competitive analysis. Identify your competitors\' strengths and weaknesses. Understand their positioning and where the gaps are in the market.

### 3. Define Your Unique Value Proposition (UVP)

- Problem Statement: What makes your product or service unique?

- Solution: Clearly articulate the unique benefits and value your product or service provides. Ensure this differentiates you from your competitors.

### 4. Craft Your Positioning Statement

- Problem Statement: How do you communicate your value?

- Solution: Create a concise positioning statement. Use this formula:

  - For \[target audience\], \[product/service\] is the \[category/market\] that \[benefit/solves problem\] because \[reason to believe\].

- Example: For busy professionals, our meal delivery service is the fastest and healthiest option that saves time and boosts well-being because of our unique quick-cook technology and dietitian-approved recipes.

### 5. Test and Refine

- Problem Statement: Is your positioning resonating with your audience?

- Solution: Test your positioning statement and messaging with a small segment of your target audience. Gather feedback and refine as necessary. Use A/B testing in your marketing campaigns to see what works best.

### Practical Tips:

- Consistency is Key: Ensure that your positioning is consistent across all marketing channels and customer touchpoints.

- Evolve as Needed: Stay adaptable. As your market and business evolve, so should your positioning.

- Internal Buy-In: Make sure your team understands and can communicate the positioning clearly.

This framework should help you create a strong positioning strategy for your startup.
