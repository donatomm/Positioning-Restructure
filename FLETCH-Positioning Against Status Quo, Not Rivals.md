Using **Vanta** (a SOC 2 compliance platform) as a case study, the document outlines why competitive messaging often misses the mark when a category is new.

## 1. The Trap: Founder Fear and Ego

When launching a product, founders often look at other startups in their space and feel the need to position against them. This leads to \"me-too\" messaging that focuses on being:

- The most **advanced**.

- The **fastest**.

- The most **comprehensive**.

- Backed by prestigious accelerators like **YC**.

**The Problem:** If the customer doesn\'t know the category exists, they aren\'t shopping for it or budgeting for it. In this context, messaging that compares \"Platform A\" to \"Platform B\" is worthless.

## 2. Redefining the Competition

The document asserts that if customers haven\'t heard of your category, your rivals are not your competition. Instead, your competition is the **status quo**---how they currently handle the **Job-To-Be-Done (JTBD)**.

For Vanta, the JTBD is **\"Completing a SOC 2 Audit\"**. The real competitors are:

- **Compliance Consultants:** High cost (e.g., \$100k).

- **In-house Manual Work:** Using spreadsheets, which is \"way more work\" than expected.

## 3. Effective Positioning Strategy

Instead of fighting other software vendors, you should position your product against the pain points of the status quo.

### The Shift in Messaging

  ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Wrong Focus (Rivals)**            **Right Focus (Status Quo)**
  ----------------------------------- ----------------------------------------------------
  \"Most advanced SOC 2 Platform\"    \"Simplifies the complex, time-consuming process\"

  \"Fastest automation platform\"     \"Addresses the tedious preparation for an audit\"

  \"Built for modern teams\"          Focuses on the outcome: the SOC 2 Audit
  ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Summary of the \"Fletch\" Philosophy

- **Ignore the \"Category\" Ego:** Don\'t assume customers care about your software category.

- **Identify the JTBD:** Determine exactly what the customer is trying to achieve.

- **Attack the Status Quo:** Your messaging resonates when it offers a better way to do the job than their current manual or expensive methods.
