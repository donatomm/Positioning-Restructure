**Why Early-Stage Startups Must Choose Their Competitors Wisely (Competition, positioning)**

**Robert Kaminski 🎯\**
*Co-Founder @ Fletch \| Positioning & Messaging for B2B Startups*

If you're trying to position your startup BUT aren't willing to pick specific competitive alternatives to compete with...\
You're going to end up competing with everything. 😱

And when you compete with everything, explaining your differentiation is near impossible --- because your product holds different value relative to the other products you're getting compared to.

This concept is crucial to understand for products that have many use cases. (ie. Airtable)

---------

What's Airtable's differentiation against a no-code automation tool?\
→ It can handle all the automation tasks but gives you more control over managing the data.

What's Airtable's differentiation against a spreadsheet?\
→ It does everything a spreadsheet does but also reliably stores data like a relational database.

What's Airtable's differentiation against a project management tool?\
→ It does everything a project management tool does but gives you the ability to customize the workflows without code completely.

👉 Their differentiation is COMPLETELY DIFFERENT when referenced against a unique alternative.

And Airtable has dozens of other tools it could compete with (CRMs, project management software, collaboration tools, etc.)

❌ Airtable can't possibly explain its unique value against all of these tools at once --- especially when they were an early-stage startup with limited resources.

They had to be selective on which tools to compete with. (and so does your early-stage startup)

They chose spreadsheets & databases.

"We're part spreadsheet, part database."\
"We're a database, but you don't need to be a developer to use it."\
"We're like a spreadsheet, but with a lot of added functionality."

These two reference points acted as shorthand for Airtable, drastically reducing the time for someone to understand what the product is, what they might use it for, and the value it could provide.

Nowadays, Airtable's positioning has matured into a multi-pronged set of positionings that speaks to the unique value against all of these alternative tools. (though they primarily position as a no-code application builder)

---------

The takeaway for early-stage startups...\
You get to decide where and who you'll compete with.\
Founders and marketers should make these decisions to highlight their unique differentiation.\
If you never decide, you'll effectively remove your ability to differentiate.
