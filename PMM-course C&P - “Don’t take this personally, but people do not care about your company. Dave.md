**Dave Gerhardt** **Founder: ExitFive.com \| Dad x2 \| Former CMO. Building the top community for marketers.**

Don\'t take this personally, but people do not care about your company.

They don\'t care about your product and your features.

They don\'t care about your new integration with Slack and HubSpot.

What they care about is themselves.

And if you can talk to them about themselves, you\'ll have their full attention.

How can you help them? What is the selfish benefit? Here are five timeless principles you can think about for your customers:

1.  Make Me Look Good

Whether it\'s to a boss, their team, or their industry, people are wired to care about status. Can you help them impress someone? Can you give them an edge? Can they copy/paste your idea and look smart in the next meeting? I remember impressing my boss when I was 23 year old marketer being asked to build a marketing plan for a product launch for the first time. I had no idea what to do, found a great resource from HubSpot blog (the OG) and used it. She was impressed.

2.  Save Me Time

Time is the one thing nobody has enough of. If you can help someone do something faster, with less friction or fewer steps, that\'s the way. People will pay for time savings. BUT they have to believe this. Can\'t just be a marketing claim. You need proof. Show the receipts. The examples.

3.  Make Me Money

Capitalism seems to be the best system that exists in the world. I don\'t make the rules I am just observing. It doesn\'t seem like someone has created a better way, so yes - money matters. And whether they will admit it or not, your customers want to make more money (because they are people). Whether directly or indirectly, people are tuned in to ROI. If you can help someone drive revenue, get more leads, reduce churn, or land a raise then you\'ve got their attention. Again though: MUST BE BELIEVABLE. So you must have PROOF.

4.  Help Me Avoid Pain

We are wired to avoid pain. We\'d almost always rather avoid pain than get some type of gain. No one likes to look stupid, feel behind, or get in trouble. If you can speak to something they\'re secretly worried about \-- a mistake, a missed goal, a clunky process \-- and show a way out, they\'ll lean in.

5.  Help Me Grow

Great marketing often speaks to ambition. Career growth. Mastery. Confidence. People want to feel like they\'re getting better --- at their job, in their life, as a person.

There\'s a lot of noise in marketing right now because of tech/AI, but I\'m more bullish than ever on understanding the timeless principles in marketing, and that starts with understanding people.

I hope these are helpful for whatever you\'re working on right now.
